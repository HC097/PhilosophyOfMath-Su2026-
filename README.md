# Philosophy of Mathematics Lecture Notes

This repository contains lecture notes for a course in the philosophy of mathematics.

The notes are designed for classroom teaching, especially lectures that make active use of a whiteboard. They are not intended to be polished textbook chapters or fully scripted lectures. Instead, they provide structured teaching materials: central themes, key distinctions, whiteboard prompts, teaching points, discussion questions, and conceptual summaries.

## Course Aim

The course investigates foundational questions about mathematics, including:

- What is mathematics about?
- Do mathematical objects exist?
- How is mathematical knowledge possible?
- Is mathematics discovered or invented?
- What is the relationship between mathematics and logic?
- Why is mathematics applicable to the empirical sciences?
- Should philosophy guide mathematical practice, or should it interpret mathematics as practiced?

The lectures are based on readings in the philosophy of mathematics and are organized chapter by chapter.
